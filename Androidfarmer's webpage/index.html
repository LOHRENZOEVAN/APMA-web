Problem

Recurring animal disease outbreaks caused significant economic losses of up to $200 million in the past 10 years in Africa, trade disruptions, and threats to global food security. Smallholder animal farmers in Africa, with already fragile systems, were disproportionately affected by these outbreaks. Targeted interventions are crucial to manage and mitigate the impact of livestock diseases, particularly focusing on prevalent diseases like Avian Influenza, Foot and Mouth Disease, and African Swine Fever.
